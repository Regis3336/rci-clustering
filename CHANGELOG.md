# Changelog

All notable changes to **RCI: Reverse Clustering Impact** are documented here.  
This project follows a structural-behavioral versioning logic rather than strict semantic versioning. Version numbers mark changes in the mathematical identity, operational behavior, documentation, and reproducibility surface of the framework.

---

## [Unreleased]

Planned extensions and research directions:

- **Graph-RCI:** extension of canonical cells, farthest-point covers, radial Morse profiles, and curvature signatures to general weighted graphs.

## [1.3.0] — Canonical Cells, Canonical Homology, and Nerve Moduli — 2026-05-20

### Added

- **Canonical RCI cells:**  
  - Introduced explicit language of canonical RCI cells $C_j^\star$;
  - Each generated by: algorithmic center $c_j$; local radial profile $M_{c_j}(k)$; boundary index $\kappa_j^\star$; canonical local radius $r_j^\star$;
  - Clarified "cluster" as implementation/legacy terminology, "cell" as mathematically precise object.

- **Canonical radii:**  
  - Elevated $r_j^\star$ to central geometric object of RCI;
  - Not externally tuned;
  - Each $r_j^\star$ extracted from first admissible Morse-type transition of corresponding radial profile;
  - Different RCI cells may carry different intrinsic canonical radii.

- **Canonical homology:**  
  - Added distinction between canonical and dynamic homology;
  - Primary RCI output carries canonical homological information;
  - Generated by locally calibrated cell system and its canonical radii;
  - Belongs to primary RCI output, not to be confused with Čech nerve layer.

- **Dynamic homology:**  
  - Added explicit dynamic layer obtained by radial scale exploration;
  - Thickening or radially varying canonical cells produces changing gluing regimes;
  - Changing gluing regimes generate dynamic homology across scale.

- **RCI nerve moduli:**  
  - Introduced term **RCI nerve moduli**;
  - RCI does not output nontrivial Čech nerve at canonical level;
  - Nontrivial Čech nerve appears only after additional scale operation;
  - Each $N(\lambda)$ is a scale-section of the RCI nerve moduli;
  - Relevant topological object is scale-indexed family
    $$
    \{N(\lambda)\}_{\lambda\in\Lambda}.
    $$

- **Canonical versus dynamic hierarchy:**  
  Added final conceptual hierarchy:

  ```text
  RCI canonical layer:
  points + metric
  → canonical RCI cells
  → canonical radii
  → canonical partition / canonical cover
  → canonical homology

  Radial scale exploration:
  canonical RCI cells
  → changing gluing regimes
  → dynamic homology
  → RCI nerve moduli
  → Čech nerves as scale-sections
  ```

- **Scale-section terminology:**
  - Added terminology "scale-section $N(\lambda)$" for individual nerve from nerve moduli;
  - "The RCI nerve" is not primitive object; primitive dynamic object is the nerve moduli.

- **Core identity of RCI clarified:**  
  - Reframed as metric radial procedure on finite metric spaces;
  - Not spectral, graph, or density-threshold clustering;
  - Transforms metric data into locally calibrated cells and canonical homological information.

- **Metric core versus spectral implementation:**  
  - Separated mathematical definition from spectral implementation;
  - Radial orderings, density estimates, local profiles, finite differences, boundary indices, canonical radii depend only on supplied metric;
  - Spectral embeddings, matrix-on-demand Laplacians, kNN graphs are computational workspaces, not definition of RCI;
  - Spectral chart allowed as operational Euclidean representation, does not redefine algorithm.

- **Matrix-on-demand Laplacian:**  
  - Reframed as matrix-on-demand, not globally materialized adjacency/Laplacian matrix;
  - Local stencils provide entries only when requested;
  - Acts functionally through matrix-vector calls;
  - Purpose: efficient local metric access and scalable spectral querying.

- **Algorithmic centers:**  
  - Distinguished analytic centers from algorithmic centers;
  - Fixed centers $c$ used in proofs for local analysis;
  - Actual centers $c_j$ generated by RCI residual seeding dynamics;
  - First-center rule:
    $$
    c_1 \in \arg\max_{z_i}\|z_i-\bar z\|;
    $$
  - Residual surface rule:
    $$
    s_J(x) = \min_{1 \le j \le J} \max\{0, d(x, c_j) - r_j^\star\};
    $$
  - Added metric initialization alternative:
    $$
    \operatorname{rem}(x) = \frac{1}{|X|-1} \sum_{y \ne x} d(x, y).
    $$

- **Boundary index:**  
  - Updated admissible warm-up condition from $m_0 \ge 2$ to $m_0 \ge 3$;
  - $m_0 \ge 3$ avoids degenerate centered second-difference indices;
  - Strengthened well-posedness theorem for each selected center;
  - Added deterministic tie-breaking assumptions;
  - Clarified fallback behavior when no admissible concavity-change index exists;
  - $\kappa_j^\star$ determines the canonical radius $r_j^\star$.

- **RCI profile:**  
  - Theoretical RCI profile:
    $$
    A_c(k) = \frac{1}{k+1} \sum_{x \in B_k(c)} \phi(\widehat{\rho}(x)), \quad M_c(k) = A_c(k) - \alpha \log r_k;
    $$
  - Theoretical guarantees stated for logarithmic contrast $\phi(t) = \log t$;
  - Separated theoretical logarithmic density contrast from benchmark-level numerical stabilization;
  - Documented epsilon-regularized radii in implementation contexts;
  - Implementation-level smoothing and refinement preserve Morse interpretation while improving finite-sample robustness.

- **Scaling invariance:**  
  - Positive metric rescaling shifts $M_c(k)$ by a constant independent of $k$;
  - First and second differences are invariant under positive metric scaling;
  - Boundary indices and labels unchanged under positive rescaling;
  - Removed or avoided unsupported claims of full affine invariance;
  - Transformations $d'(x,y) = a d(x,y) + b$ with $b > 0$ do not generally preserve the RCI profile.

- **Dimensional neutrality:**  
  - $\alpha = m$ cancels leading volumetric term;
  - Framed RCI as dimensionally neutral after volumetric correction;
  - Operates in high-dimensional or effectively infinite-dimensional Hilbert-like settings when local metric regularity is present;
  - Mechanism depends on metric behavior, local radii, and radial profiles, not on explicit ambient dimension.

- **Curvature law:**  
  - Strengthened curvature interpretation of second difference:
    $$
    \mathbb{E}[\Delta^2 M_c(k)] \approx -C_m S_g(c) \Delta(r_k^2);
    $$
  - Sign behavior: positive curvature tends to produce negative second difference; negative curvature tends to produce positive second difference;
  - Smooth Riemannian derivation is a model case;
  - Mechanism extends to locally regular metric-measure settings;
  - $\Delta^2 M_c(k)$ is a Morse-type curvature signal, not a mere density statistic.

- **RCI versus density clustering:**  
  - Strengthened distinction between RCI and DBSCAN/HDBSCAN;
  - Density-based methods react primarily to sampling concentration;
  - RCI normalizes radial mass growth;
  - RCI detects Morse-type transitions in radial profiles;
  - $\Delta^2 M_c(k)$ detects change of regime, not simply point density.

- **RCI versus spectral clustering:**  
  - RCI is not spectral clustering;
  - Spectral embeddings are optional computational charts;
  - Metric procedure runs on original coordinates or spectral representation;
  - Spectral fidelity justified only where local bi-Lipschitz estimates apply;
  - Spectral layer supplies metric surrogate and does not define RCI.

- **Benchmark configuration:**  
  - Implementation class name `SpectralRCI` is historical/implementation-level;
  - Reported benchmark runs use `use_spectral=False`;
  - `n_eigenvectors=5` is inactive when `use_spectral=False`;
  - No spectral embedding used for clustering or MEI computation in reported non-spectral benchmarks.

- **MEI clarification:**  
  - MEI measures geometric fidelity, not classification accuracy;
  - MEI does not use ground-truth labels;
  - MEI evaluates preservation of Morse density field across cell boundaries;
  - RCI evaluated using neighborhood structure generated by its own run;
  - Classical clustering methods return partitions only;
  - Classical methods evaluated on common post-hoc mutual kNN graph;
  - Difference between RCI’s internal neighborhood structure and external benchmark scaffolds.

- **Multi-scale profile $M(r)$:**  
  - Farthest-point covers define multi-scale profile $M(r)$;
  - $M(r)$ comparable to box-counting function $N(r)$;
  - Role of $M(r)$ as global metric-complexity profile;
  - $M(r)$ is not merely a cluster-count curve;
  - Global transitions in $M(r)$ align with discrete Morse transitions;
  - $M(r)$ records scale-dependent geometric complexity.

- **Fractal and box-counting layer:**  
  - Strengthened relationship between RCI and box-counting geometry;
  - Farthest-point cover counts comparable to covering numbers under doubling assumptions;
  - Strengthened fractal completeness language;
  - RCI captures metric complexity through scale-dependent covering behavior.

- **Discrete Morse scaffold:**  
  - Strengthened connection with Forman-style discrete Morse structure;
  - Farthest-point selection induces discrete Morse scaffold;
  - Critical transitions align with jumps in $M(r)$ and changes in $\Delta^2 M_c(k)$;
  - Cell formation interpreted as Morse-type transition, not merely partitioning event;
  - Boundary index functions as local Morse detector.

- **Appendix C / topological layer:**  
  - Expanded from homological interpretation into full scale/topological layer;
  - Added or strengthened: canonical homology; dynamic homology; distinction between canonical homology and Čech nerve layer;
  - Scale poset; Alexandroff topology; sheaf of finite RCI states; separatedness; gluing; deterministic merge maps;
  - Spectral cover; Čech nerve at each scale-section; compatibility under merges; type-A persistence module;
  - Interval decomposition; sheafified radial barcode; $H_0$ recovery of merge profile; RCI nerve theorem;
  - Safe comparative morphism; computational validation of sheaf, nerve, and barcode behavior.

- **Sheaf layer:**  
  - Defined over scale poset, not over arbitrary metric opens;
  - Arbitrary metric-open sheafification fails in general;
  - Spectral Laplacian eigenvectors are global and can violate locality over arbitrary opens;
  - Scale evolution under merging is deterministic and local in scale variable;
  - Sheaf axioms hold over scale poset because restriction and merge maps are deterministic;
  - Scale poset organizes dynamic layer, not primary canonical partition.

- **Čech nerve layer:**  
  - Defined only at scale-sections of RCI nerve moduli;
  - Each $N(\lambda)$ comes from induced spectral/geometric cover at that scale;
  - Primary canonical cell system is not itself a nontrivial Čech nerve;
  - Spectral balls are convex/contractible in embedding space;
  - Compatibility assumptions under merge maps;
  - Nerve theorem applies to finite cover at selected scale under stated assumptions;
  - Čech nerve is representational layer for dynamic gluing regimes.

- **Betti-number caution:**  
  - Betti numbers computed by RCI nerve pipeline are Betti numbers of finite RCI nerve at selected scale;
  - Not automatically Betti numbers of ambient smooth manifold;
  - Manifold-level interpretation requires hypotheses of nerve correspondence;
  - Prevented overclaiming from finite nerve homology to ambient topology;
  - Canonical homology and scale-section Betti numbers belong to different layers.

- **Persistence module:**  
  - Strengthened type-A persistence module formulation;
  - Scale-indexed nerves induce persistence maps;
  - Interval decomposition gives sheafified radial barcode;
  - Barcode information reflects evolution of gluing regimes among RCI cells;
  - Persistence belongs to dynamic layer;
  - Dynamic homology is homological evolution induced by radial exploration.

- **Appendix D / robustness architecture:**  
  - Reorganized robustness proofs while preserving original mathematical narrative and named ingredients;
  - Preserved original concentration structure based on bounded differences and McDiarmid’s inequality;
  - Preserved original geometric bootstrapping architecture for curvature law;
  - Added or strengthened: spectral workspace convention; regularity assumptions; annulus population assumptions;
  - Davis–Kahan displacement control; McDiarmid concentration; good-geometry event; shell-average concentration;
  - Radius concentration; second-difference stability; admissibility clarification; Gray–Vanhecke volume expansion;
  - Log-bias of $k$-NN density on a manifold; curvature law proof.

- **Robustness proof architecture:**  
  - Preserved original proof architecture of Appendix D as main body;
  - Used later revisions only as local patches where they clarified argument;
  - Removed proof-sketch language from technical appendix;
  - Boundary detection evaluated only on admissible non-degenerate indices;
  - Role of shell averages, bounded differences, and local metric regularity;
  - Perturbation bounds transfer to second-difference stability.

- **Computational reproducibility:**  
  - Strengthened reproducibility language;
  - Benchmark scripts use fixed seeds;
  - Implementation, test suite, figures, and benchmark pipelines reproducible from companion repository;
  - Distinction between computational validation of framework behavior and formal proof of each lemma;
  - Experiments validate end-to-end predicted behavior, while proofs carry mathematical claims;
  - Computational experiments do not replace peer review or formal verification.

- **AI-assisted methodology section:**

  - AI systems assisted with formalization, critique, and reconstruction;
  - Final mathematical responsibility remains with author;
  - Computational validation and adversarial review are not substitutes for peer review;

  - Paper remains open to external correction and review.
